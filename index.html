Welcome Fiber Artists

No pattern, no problem

Crocheting freehand can be an exciting, yet frustrating process, especially for those wanting to design and sell their own patterns. Sometimes the creative block can begin before you even start your project. You have to decide what to make, the colors, the style, everything down to the very last stitch. This page is designed to help you get started on your next project and help work through creative blocks!

    Here are some tips and tricks to starting your project!
  • Choose your yarn.

    If you are making a festive item, choose the appropriate colors to match the occasion. Make sure to select the appropriate yarn weight, using fine weight yarn to make a potholder may not be the best idea...

  • Gather the proper tools

    Okay, so you've got your yarn, now you need a crochet hook (There is always a size recommendation listed on the back of the skein of yarn), scissors, and a darning needle (for sewing and weaving ends).

  • Additional Materials

    Depending on your project, there are some additional items that could be needed such as fiber fill, safety eyes, buttons, or stitch markers.

  • Do your research

    When crocheting without a pattern, it can be easy to get lost in your stitches. Make sure to look up any foreign stitches or techniques to help prevent mistakes.

  • It's Okay to Unravel

    Mistakes happen, and good craftsmanship takes time and patience. If a mistake is noticable and messes up the rest of your work, it is okay to go back and fix it.

  • Write Everything Down

    • If you are crocheting freehand, it is crucial that you write down everything you do. Write down how many stitches are in each row, which stitches you're using,
